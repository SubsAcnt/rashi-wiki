# Planets in Vedic Astrology

The nine planets, or Navagrahas, are the fundamental components of Vedic astrology. Each planet has unique characteristics, significations, and influences.

## The Navagrahas

The nine planets are:

1. **Sun (Surya)** - The soul and ego
2. **Moon (Chandra)** - The mind and emotions
3. **Mars (Mangal)** - Energy and courage
4. **Mercury (Budha)** - Intellect and communication
5. **Jupiter (Guru)** - Wisdom and expansion
6. **Venus (Shukra)** - Love and luxury
7. **Saturn (Shani)** - Discipline and karma
8. **Rahu** - Desires and material pursuits
9. **Ketu** - Spirituality and detachment

## Classification

### Natural Benefics
- Jupiter, Venus, Mercury (when with benefics), Moon (waxing)

### Natural Malefics
- Sun, Mars, Saturn, Rahu, Ketu

### Luminaries
- Sun and Moon are considered luminaries rather than planets

## Planetary Characteristics

Each planet rules:

- **Two signs** (except Sun and Moon which rule one each)
- **Specific houses** based on their natural significations
- **Certain periods** in the dasha system
- **Various aspects** of human life

## Strength Factors

Planetary strength is determined by:

- Position in signs (exaltation, debilitation, own sign)
- House placement (angular, trinal, etc.)
- Aspects from other planets
- Conjunctions and relationships
- Shadbala calculations

## Dasha Periods

Each planet rules specific periods in various dasha systems:

- **Vimshottari Dasha** - 120-year cycle
- **Yogini Dasha** - 36-year cycle
- Other regional dasha systems
