# Sun (Surya) in Vedic Astrology

The Sun, known as Surya in Sanskrit, is the king of all planets and represents the soul, ego, authority, and vitality.

## Basic Information

- **Sanskrit Name**: Surya
- **Nature**: Malefic (but can be benefic in certain positions)
- **Element**: Fire
- **Gender**: Male
- **Own Signs**: Leo (Simha)
- **Exaltation**: Aries (10 degrees)
- **Debilitation**: Libra (10 degrees)
- **Moolatrikona**: Leo (0-20 degrees)

## Significations

The Sun represents:

- **Soul and Self**: The core identity and ego
- **Authority**: Government, father, boss, leadership
- **Vitality**: Health, energy, life force
- **Fame and Recognition**: Public image, reputation
- **Eyes**: Vision and eye health
- **Bones**: Skeletal structure
- **Heart**: Cardiovascular system

## House Significations

The Sun is particularly significant for:

- **1st House**: Strong personality, leadership qualities
- **4th House**: Relationship with father, property matters
- **5th House**: Children, creativity, education
- **9th House**: Father, dharma, higher learning
- **10th House**: Career, authority, public recognition

## Strength Factors

The Sun is strong when:

- Placed in Leo (own sign)
- In Aries (exaltation)
- In angular houses (1st, 4th, 7th, 10th)
- In 10th house (karma bhava)
- During daytime birth
- In Shukla Paksha (waxing moon phase)

## Weakness Factors

The Sun is weak when:

- Placed in Libra (debilitation)
- In 6th, 8th, or 12th houses
- Combust (too close to Sun)
- During nighttime birth
- In Krishna Paksha (waning moon phase)

## Dasha Period

- **Vimshottari Dasha**: 6 years
- **Effects**: Periods of authority, recognition, health focus, relationship with father

## Remedies

- Worship on Sundays
- Chanting Surya mantras
- Wearing ruby (Manikya)
- Fasting on Sundays
- Donating wheat, copper, or red items
